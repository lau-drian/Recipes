## Ingredients
- 100 g carrots
- 100 g cabbage
- 100 g zucchini
- A handful of baby spinach, coarsely chopped
- 1 ½ tsp salt
- 100 g chickpea (gram) flour
- 4 Tbsp rice flour (or more chickpea flour)
- ½ tsp baking powder
- 2 tsp nigella seeds
- 1 tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp garam masala
- ½ tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp freshly ground cardamom
- 4 Tbsp water (or olive oil for the baked version)

## Steps
1. Grate the cabbage, carrot and zucchini and place in a bowl with the salt.
2. Knead and squeeze the vegetables for a minute or two until they release their liquid, then add the spinach.
3. Stir together the chickpea flour, rice flour, baking powder and all the spices in a small bowl.
4. Add the flour mixture to the vegetables along with the water (use oil for the baked version). Mix until everything is coated; the consistency should be like a dry muffin batter. Add a little extra water or oil if needed.
5. **Fried:** fill a small frying pan with high edges with a heat-proof oil and heat over medium. Add large tablespoons of batter and fry 1-2 min per side until golden and crispy. Drain on kitchen paper.
6. **Baked:** preheat the oven to **225 °C**. Drop 12 large tablespoons of batter onto a lined tray, sprinkle with nigella seeds and bake for 18-20 min, until golden and crispy.
